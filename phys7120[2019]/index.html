This is continuation of Phys 7120 + Astr/Phys 7130 course for Spring 2019 semester. The preceeding material can be found at Prof. Zheng Zheng's web page.

Textbooks: The textbook for the class is George B. Rybicki and Alan P. Lightman, Radiative Processes in Astrophysics. The books by Ch. A. Brau, Modern Problems in Classical Electrodynamics, by L. D. Landau and E. M. Lifshitz, The Theory of Fields (volume 2 of their Theoretical Physics course), and by D. B. Melrose and R. C. McPhedran, Electromagnetic processes in dispersive media, are helpful (but not required). Much of the material will be presented in the form of lecture notes.

Course Description Electrodynamics II is the second part of the year-long graduate course. The approximate outline is as follows:

■ Radiation by relativistic particls.

■ Wave propagation in medium.

■ Elements of plasma theory.

Course requirements and expectations Lecture attendance is expected. No late homeworks are accepted unless there is an explicit agreement with the instructor regarding this. Students are expected to do most of the course-related work outside the class. Working through assigned reading should take as much time as the scheduled lectures, and it will take at least a few hours to do the average homework.

Homeworks are due on Mondays at 9:40 am (before the start of the class). There will be no homeworks in the weeks immediately after a midterm.

Grading policy and exams: homework 60%; midterm exam 15%; final exam 25%; no make-up test/assignments unless for legitimate reasons: emergency (documented), university-approved travel, etc.

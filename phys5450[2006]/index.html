Course Description:

The quantum mechanics course is designed to give the student working knowledge of quantum mechanics. The theoretical underpinning are broad enough and deep enough to support later developments, while the applications are quite concrete and practical. In particular, the examples are chosen to demonstrate the wide applicability of the ideas of quantum mechanics, while at the same time informally presenting the important approximation techniques needed to make use of quantum mechanics.

Course PRE-REQUISITES: PHYCS 3740, 4420; MATH 3150, 3160.

Required Text:

David J. Griffiths, Introduction to Quantum Mechanics (Second Edition), Publisher: Prentice Hall (March 31, 2004), ISBN: 0-13-111892-7 .
Several copies are available in the Physics Department Library: you can check it out for several hours from Megan Bennett, in room 201-A JFB (university ID is required).

Recommended Texts:

  • A Modern Approach to Quantum Mechanics, John S. Townsend, University Science Books, 2000; ISBN 1891389130 . One copy is available on Reserve in Marriott Library.
  • The Feynman Lectures on Physics, vol.3, Quantum Mechanics, Addison Wesley Longman (1970); ISBN 0201021153 . The classic and inspiring reading. Townsend's book is largely motivated by Feynman's.
  • Modern Quantum Mechanics, J.J. Sakurai, Addison Wesley Longman (1994); ISBN 0-201-53929-2 (this book is used in higher level quantum courses Phycs 7220 and 7230).
  • Quantum Mechanics (non-relativistic theory), L.D. Landau and E.M. Lifshitz, Butterworth-Heinemann, 3rd edition (1981); ISBN 0750635398 . A must for advanced theory students.

    Course Format:

    Class meets on Tuesdays and Thursdays for 100-minutes long lectures. These are followed by Discussion Session on Friday, which is led by the Teaching Assistant. Discussion Session is organized to help you with the material covered in the preceeding lectures (usually this is done by working one-two problems from the textbook in details) and to answer your questions on homework problems.

    Homework:

    Weekly problem sets will be required and will count toward grades. Generally, we'll have one homework per week. Homework schedule can be found in the class Schedule which you should check regularly for posted homework assignments, additional suggested reading materials, as well as homework solutions which will be posted there every week.
  • Homeworks are to be turned in on Fridays, by 4 p.m.
    Discussions of homework problems are encouraged. However, every homework must be written individually. Copying other person's solutions is prohibited.

    Grading Policy:

    Two midterm exams and a final will be administered. All exams are open book, open notes. The (tentative) grading scheme is as follows:
  • Homework 40%
  • Midterms 30% (15% each)
  • Final Exam 30%
      Please note: If you have a problem with how a homework or an exam is graded, you must see me (not the grader) within one week from the date of the grade . I will not consider questions on re-grading of any kind after one week time.

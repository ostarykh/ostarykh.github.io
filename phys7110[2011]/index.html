Course Description The course consists of two parts.

1) CLASSICAL MECHANICS covers: Calculus of variations and the least action principles for classical mechanics and geometrical optics. Lagrangian equations. Hamiltonian formulation of classical mechanics. Einstein's principle of relativity and four-dimensional derivation of relativistic mechanics from the least action principle.

2) ELECTRODYNAMICS (part I) covers: Electrostatics and Magnetostatics in vacuum and in the continuous media. Core Maxwell's equations and material equations. Different types of material equations. Methods of solution of static problems. Quasi-Static Electrodynamics in conducting media.

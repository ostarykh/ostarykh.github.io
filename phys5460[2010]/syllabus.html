Syllabus

Topics to be covered:

  • Probability Theory: Probability densities, cumulants and correlations; central limit theorem.
  • Thermodynamics: Thermal equilibrium, the laws of thermodynamics; temperature, energy, entropy, and other functions of state.
  • Classical Statistical Mechanics: Postulates; microcanonical, canonical and grand canonical ensembles; non-interacting examples.
  • Interacting Systems: Virial and cluster expansions; van der Waals theory; liquid-vapor condensation.
  • Quantum Statistical Mechanics: Quantization effects in molecular gases; phonons, photons.
  • Identical Particles: Degenerate quantum gases; Fermi liquids; Bose condensation; superfluidity.
  • Elements of kinetic theory.
    • The course is based on Kardar's book "Statistical Mechanics of Particles" and MIT OpenCourseWare course Statistical Mechanics I (8.333) taught by Prof. Mehran Kardar. Much of the material will be presented in a slightly different way, and the corresponding lecture notes will be posted in the Schedule section of the class webpage.

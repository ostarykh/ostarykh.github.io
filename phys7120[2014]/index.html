Course Description Electrodynamics II is a second part of the year-long graduate course. In departure from the traditional routine of two long lectures per week, there will normally be 2 shorter lectures (Mon, Wed), taught by Prof. Oleg Starykh, and 2 seminar-style discussions (Tue, Thu), led by Prof. Eugene Mishchenko. The emphasis of the discussions is on active problem-solving under the direction of instructors.

Homeworks are due on Wednesdays at 11:50 am (before the start of the class). There will be no homeworks in the weeks immediately after a midterm.

Main topics to be covered: field of moving charges, radiation, waveguides, and electrodynamics of linear, dispersive media.

Previous semester's web page (Fall 2013, Electrodynamics I) can be found here.

Course requirements Lecure and discussion section attendance is expected. No late homeworks are accepted unless there is an explicit agreement with the instructor regarding this.

Grading policy and exams: 10 highest-score homeworks, 15 points each (33.3%); 2 midterms, 75 points each (33.3%); final test 150 points (33.3%); no make-up test/assignments unless for legitimate reasons: emergency (documented), university-approved travel, etc.

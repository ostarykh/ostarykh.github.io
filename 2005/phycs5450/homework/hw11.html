    PROBLEM 1: Cosider a four-state system where states |n> (n=1,2,3,4) have energies E_0, and amplitude to hope to the nearest-neighbors is -A. The states are arranged in the loop which can be visualized as 1->2->3->4->1, or as a square made of four sites. (This is just an extention of the 3-state system analyzed in the class. If the set-up is not clear, consult your lecture notes.) Following the strategy developed in the class, set up the eigenvalue problem and find (a) eigenvalues E [15 pts.], (b) eigenstates [15 pts.]. (c) normalize your eigenstates; choose an arbitrary pair of them and show that they are indeed orthogonal to each other [10 pts.]. (d) the translation operator T is defined via T|n> = |n+1>. Show that each of the obtained eigenstates is also an eigenstate of that operator, and derive the corresponding eigenvalues [10 pts.].

    PROBLEM 2: For the one-dimensional lattice problem analyzed in the class, consider the effect of adding the amplitude (denoted -F, with F > 0) to hop to the next-nearest-neighbor sites. In other words, this amplitude connects state |n> with its |n-2> and |n+2> neighbors. (a) Solve the eigenvalue problem and derive expression for the energy eigenvalue E(k) as a function of wavenumber k. [30 pts.] (b) Set E_0 = 0, and sketch this function for A=1, F=0 and A=1, F=1/4. What is the period of E(k) obtained? [20 pts.]

Nonlinear Fano effect in quantum dots

In atomic absorption spectra, the Fano effect is an asymmetric line arising from the interference between a discrete state and a continuum of states. Recently a similar effect has been observed in an InAs quantum dot capped with GaAs. Here the interference arises from competing absorption paths into the conduction band: one from a valence electron and the other from the valence level coupled to a 2-dimensional valence band arising from the GaAs cap. More intense radiation enhances the second path, producing a clear Fano line shape. We refer to this behavior as a nonlinear Fano effect [1]. The essence of the new effect is that even a very weak dot-continuum interaction can become apparent in the nonlinear regime. If a continuum contains a Fermi sea, an exciton becomes hybridized [2]. In the filled-continuum regime, an exciton may have a short spin lifetime or form a Kondo state. Fano lineshapes may also appear due to the Coulomb coupling between excitons in semiconductor dots and plasmons in metal nanocrystals.

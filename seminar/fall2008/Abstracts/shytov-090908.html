Condensed Matter Seminar

Andrey Shytov
University of Utah

Tuesday, September 9, 2008; 4:00 pm in JFB 334

Relativistic dynamics in graphene: Klein paradox and atomic collapse.

Electrons in graphene, a novel two-dimensional material, behave similarly to Dirac particles. Quantum relativistic effects defying common intuition are manifested in unusual transport properties of this system. In this talk, I will concentrate on two such phenomena. Klein paradox (perfect transmission of relativistic particles through high barriers) is relevant for properties of pn- and pnp-junctions in graphene. Another relativistic effect, atomic collapse, may be relevant for understanding the reconstruction of Dirac vacuum near Coulomb impurities in graphene.

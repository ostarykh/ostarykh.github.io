Recent progress in the use of fully correlated exponentials for the description of four-body Coulomb systems

Coulomb problems involving three or four particles can advantageously be described using wavefunctions which explicitly involve all the interparticle distances rij. The most effective basis sets for these problems involve exponentials in all the rij, and analytical methods for evaluating their matrix elements have become available. Alternatively, these systems can be studied using Gaussians in the rij; the most efficient Gaussian basis sets will need members in which the rij also occur to odd powers.

We review the current status of this area of research, commenting on

  • Analytical methods for three and four-body integrals;
  • The unexpectedly high accuracy of compact wavefunctions, illustrated by results for the He isoelectronic series;
  • Treatment of the individually divergent integrals that occur in singularity-cancelling combinations when various properties are evaluated;
  • Hypervirial theorems and their potential use for assessing wavefunction quality.

    • Results are presented for studies on the Li atom (an ``adiabatic'' system) and Ps2 (a ``nonadiabatic'' system consisting of two electrons and two positrons). For Li, for which many more extensive computations are available, we find correlated exponential wavefunctions to provide the most rapidly convergent approach thus far reported.

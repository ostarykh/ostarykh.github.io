Condensed Matter Seminar

Olexei Motrunich

California Institute of Technology

Tuesday, December 2, 2008; 4:00 pm in JFB 334

Spinon Fermi sea state as a candidate spin liquid in weak Mott insulators: studies of triangular spin model with ring exchanges.

Organic materials κ-(ET)2Cu2(CN)3 and EtMe3Sb[Pd(dmit)2]2 have emerged recently as experimental realizations of spin liquids. Both are half-filled triangular lattice systems just on the Mott insulator side of the metal-insulator transition. I will argue for an appropriate spin-1/2 model with ring exchanges and propose a candidate spin liquid with Fermi surface of spinons. This is realized as a Gutzwiller-projected Fermi sea and is a good variational state in the ring exchange model. I will discuss an effective description of this unusual insulator phase and review a number of predictions for experiments such as metall-like spin susceptibility and specific heat at low temperatures. While this proposal has an appealing phenomenology, establishing the true behavior even in the concrete spin model is a daunting task. I will describe our efforts to access such 2D spin liquids by controlled numerical studies of the ring model on ladders looking for quasi-1D descendant phases, where we find that already a 2-leg ladder shows a prominent quasi-1D descendant of the spin liquid.

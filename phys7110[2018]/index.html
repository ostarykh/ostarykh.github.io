Textbooks: The textbook for the class is Charles A. Brau, Modern Problems in Classical Electrodynamics. Classic book of Landau and Lifshitz, The Theory of Fields (volume 2 of their Theoretical Physics course), is very helpful (but is not required). Much of the material will be presented in the form of lecture notes.

Course Description Electrodynamics I is the first part of the year-long graduate course. It contains both 'Theoretical Mechanics' and 'Electrodymanics I' material. The former will take up about 1/4 of the course. The approximate outline is as follows:

■ Lagrangian formulation of classical and relativistic mechanics. Tensor analysis.

■ Relativistic particls in electromagnetic fields.

■ Relativistic field theory. Maxwell equations.

■ Static EM fields.

■ Electromagnetic waves.

Course Objectives This is a core first-year graduate class whose main objectives are: (1) to lay down the foundations of the understanding of the field theory, including development of important math skills in applications of tensor algebra, partial differential equations, vector calculus, etc., essential for physicists of all later specializations; (2) to acquire working knowledge of the broad spectrum of electromagnetic phenomena, including practical ability to analyze them qualitatively as well as quantitatively; the problems to be addressed during this course will range from classical ones in electrostatics to modern applications.

Course requirements and expectations Lecture attendance is expected. No late homeworks are accepted unless there is an explicit agreement with the instructor regarding this. Students are expected to do most of the course-related work outside the class. Working through assigned reading should take as much time as the scheduled lectures, and it will take at least a few hours to do the average homework.

Homeworks are due on Wednesdays at 10:45 am (before the start of the class). There will be no homeworks in the weeks immediately after a midterm.

Grading policy and exams: 10 highest-score homeworks, 15 points each (33.3%); 2 midterms, 75 points each (33.3%); final test 150 points (33.3%); no make-up test/assignments unless for legitimate reasons: emergency (documented), university-approved travel, etc.
